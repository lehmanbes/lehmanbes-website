Meet Our Panelists

Panel 3

Robert Kurkjian

Dr. Robert Kurkjian is the Principal of Phase 5 Environmental and a technical consultant to Tetra Tech. Robert has more than 28 years of experience conducting complex environmental investigations; evaluating surface water, ground water, and drinking water quality; remediating soil and groundwater; performing air quality studies; providing litigation support for the cleanup of Brownfield sites, and managing hazardous wastes in the U.S. and internationally. Robert also serves as a Technical Advisor to several environmental organizations and is a member of the Board of Directors of Chemists without Borders.

He is an active member of the Environmental Advisory Commission of the City of Pasadena where he provides expert advice related to energy, water, and the City’s Climate Action Plan and Zero Waste Plan. Robert earned a Ph.D. in Environmental Geochemistry from the University of California, a Master of Science degree from the New Jersey Institute of Technology, and a Bachelor of Arts in Chemistry from the University of Arizona.

Nira Rahman

Nira Rahman is an environmental planner at VHB, a civil engineering firm, where she produces environmental review reports for private developers and government agencies. Her work involves GIS analysis, writing reports, and building strong relationships with clients to ensure that development in New York City meets the standards set forth by the City Environment Quality Review in addition to state and federal guidelines. Nira earned her B.S. in Environmental Sciences from Lehman College, where is pursuing a Master’s degree in Geographic Information Sciences. Her past professional experiences include a year-long internship with the New York City Mayor’s Office of Resiliency where she developed materials to communicate flood risk and flood insurance policies for the city’s coastal communities.

She provided research assistance to a professor at Brooklyn College who was studying seahorse immunology to microbes in human-impacted environments and pristine environments. Nira also held several positions during her undergraduate career at Lehman as a GISc lab tutor, college assistant, and PEER Educator at the Office of Community Engagement. As a native New Yorker, her primary interest is to bridge science and policy as a means of mitigating environmental impacts from climate change in urban areas.

Grace M. Robiou

Grace Robiou works in the Office of Water at the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, where she has directed over the years multiple programs related to the protection of rivers, streams, lakes, wetlands, beaches, coastal areas, and drinking water.  She has over 20 years of experience protecting our nation’s waters from contamination so that people like you and I can fish, swim, recreate, drink, and generally enjoy and rely on water that is not polluted. Grace received a bachelor’s in science in Environmental Science from Tufts University, completing her coursework at Harvard University in the Visiting Undergraduate Program. She earned a master’s in public health from The Johns Hopkins University.


About the U.S. EPA and her office:

The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) exists to protect human health and the environment. EPA works to ensure that Americans have clean air, land and water. National efforts to reduce environmental risk are based on the best available scientific information, and all parts of society have access to accurate information to effectively participate in managing risks and protecting the health of communities. Environmental stewardship is integral to U.S. policies concerning economic growth, energy, transportation, agriculture, industry, international trade, and natural resources. I specifically work in the Office of Water, which ensure drinking water is safe, and restores and maintains oceans, watersheds, and their aquatic ecosystems to protect human health, support economic and recreational activities, and provide healthy habitat for fish, plants and wildlife.

Khalil Shahyd

Khalil Shahyd is a Senior Policy Advocate in the Healthy People/Thriving Communities Program at the Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC) At the NRDC he leads the organization’s Federal advocacy strategy integrating clean energy and energy efficiency with affordable housing. He has more than 20 years of experience first as a community organizer in New Orleans and later in the environmental movement. He has worked in urban and rural settings domestically and abroad in Mexico, India, and Brazil. He has a B.A. in History from Tulane University, and M.A. in Sustainable International Development from Brandeis University and a Ph.D. in Energy and


Environmental Policy from the University of Delaware with a specialization in Urban Political Ecology. Away from work, his passions are astronomy and soccer, or futebol as it's called in his second home of Brazil.

Rachel Steinberg

Rachel has led end-to-end product development in sustainability, housing, fintech, and e-commerce, with a particular passion for promoting access to opportunities. Currently, she is a Director of Product Management at Sidewalk Labs, an Alphabet company, which works to make cities more sustainable and affordable. She leads Mesa, an energy optimization engine and building IoT product to help commercial building owners, tenants, and managers tackle energy efficiency. Before working on the product team at Sidewalk, she spent many years building products globally. She split her time between India and Kenya as the Chief Product Officer at Shortlist, a job opportunities platform.


During her tenure, she led the Product, Design, Data, and Assessments teams. Prior, she was the Associate VP at Housing.com. She led 65 people to develop features to help people find homes, like recommendation engines, search, matching, and personalization. She’s also led product development in fintech and e-commerce companies, worked on financial applications at IBM, and co-found a multimedia storytelling company. Her tech focus stemmed from her work on a grassroots portfolio at the International Finance Corporation, where she witnessed the scalable impact achievable by tech-enabled businesses. Rachel loves urban planning, which she studied in undergrad alongside public policy at Princeton University. Not surprisingly, also loves tech and took as many engineering courses as possible while at Harvard Business School. She can also recommend great corner chai from her four years of living and working in India.
